The Opportunity

Why Industrial Outdoor Storage?

IOS is one of the fastest-growing sectors in commercial real estate — yet remains deeply fragmented and under-institutionalized, creating a rare arbitrage opportunity between mom-and-pop acquisition pricing and institutional exit valuations.

📍

Supply Constrained

Industrial-zoned land near population centers is finite. Zoning boards resist new IOS approvals, and existing sites are being rezoned to residential — permanently reducing supply.

📈

Demand Accelerating

E-commerce drives last-mile logistics demand. Trucking, construction, utilities, and infrastructure companies all need staging yards. The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act fuels further growth.

🛡️

Recession Resistant

IOS tenants are infrastructure and logistics companies — essential services that operate through economic cycles. Construction, utilities, and freight don't stop in downturns.

💰

Minimal CapEx, High Margins

No roofs, no HVAC, no tenant improvements. Maintenance is limited to grading, fencing, and drainage. Operating margins of 70-85% — significantly higher than traditional CRE.

Acquisition Criteria

  • ◆ Individual sites: $500K – $3M per acquisition
  • ◆ 1–10 acres, paved or improved gravel
  • ◆ Industrial zoning in place (no entitlement risk)
  • ◆ Near major interstates, rail, or logistics corridors
  • ◆ Phase I environmental clearance required

Value Creation Levers

  • ◆ Convert gross leases to Triple Net (NNN)
  • ◆ Mark rents to market (15-25% below-market typical)
  • ◆ Install 2-3% annual escalators or CPI adjustments
  • ◆ Site improvements: grading, paving, security, drainage
  • ◆ Backfill vacancy through technology partnership and broker network
